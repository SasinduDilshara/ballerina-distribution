# Asia Travel Guide

---

## Japan

**Best time to visit:** March–May (cherry blossom season) and October–November (autumn foliage)

### Visa Requirements
- Visa-free for most Western passport holders for stays up to 90 days
- IC card (Suica or Pasmo) recommended for public transport
- Cash is still preferred at many restaurants and smaller shops

### Top Destinations

#### Tokyo
- **Shibuya Crossing** — the world's busiest pedestrian intersection; best viewed from the Shibuya Sky observation deck
- **Senso-ji Temple** (Asakusa) — Tokyo's oldest Buddhist temple; visit early morning to avoid crowds
- **Akihabara** — electronics and anime culture hub
- **Tsukiji Outer Market** — fresh seafood breakfast; tuna auction requires advance reservation

#### Kyoto
- **Fushimi Inari Taisha** — thousands of vermilion torii gates winding up the mountain; hike the full trail for fewer crowds
- **Arashiyama Bamboo Grove** — arrive before 7 am to experience it without tour groups
- **Gion District** — traditional geisha district; evening strolls along Hanamikoji Street
- **Kinkaku-ji** (Golden Pavilion) — iconic Zen Buddhist temple; the most visited attraction in Kyoto

#### Osaka
- **Dotonbori** — neon-lit entertainment district famous for street food: takoyaki, okonomiyaki, kushikatsu
- **Osaka Castle** — 16th-century landmark with panoramic city views
- **Kuromon Ichiba Market** — local fresh food market, known as "Osaka's Kitchen"

### Practical Tips
- JR Pass is worth purchasing for multi-city travel including bullet trains
- Tipping is not customary and can be considered rude
- Trash cans are rare in public; carry a small bag for rubbish
- Most restaurants display plastic food models or picture menus; pointing is acceptable

## Thailand

**Best time to visit:** November–April (cool and dry season)

### Visa Requirements
- Visa-free for most nationalities for stays up to 30 days (extendable to 60 days at a land border)
- Dress modestly when visiting temples: cover shoulders and knees

### Top Destinations

#### Bangkok
- **Grand Palace** and **Wat Phra Kaew** (Temple of the Emerald Buddha) — the most visited attraction in Thailand
- **Wat Arun** — Temple of Dawn; best viewed at sunset from the opposite bank of the Chao Phraya River
- **Chatuchak Weekend Market** — one of the world's largest markets with over 15,000 stalls
- **Khao San Road** — backpacker hub for budget accommodation and nightlife

#### Chiang Mai
- **Doi Suthep Temple** — mountaintop temple with panoramic views of the city
- **Night Bazaar** — evening market with crafts, clothing, street food, and live music
- **Elephant sanctuaries** — ethical elephant encounters; avoid venues that offer elephant riding
- **Old City** — moat-surrounded historic area with over 30 ancient temples

#### Phuket and the Islands
- **Phi Phi Islands** — stunning limestone cliffs and turquoise water; accessible by speedboat from Phuket
- **Phang Nga Bay** — dramatic karst scenery; James Bond Island is the most photographed spot
- **Patong Beach** — liveliest beach with water sports and nightlife

### Practical Tips
- Bargaining is expected at markets but not in malls or restaurants
- Metered taxis and Grab (ride-hailing app) are the safest transport options
- Tap water is not safe to drink; use bottled water

## Vietnam

**Best time to visit:** Varies by region — North: September–November; Central: February–August; South: November–April

### Visa Requirements
- E-visa available for most nationalities (30-day single entry, extendable online)
- Visa-on-arrival available at major international airports

### Top Destinations

#### Hanoi
- **Hoan Kiem Lake** — serene lake in the city center with the iconic Turtle Tower and Ngoc Son Temple
- **Old Quarter** — 36 traditional trade streets; best explored on foot or by cyclo
- **Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum** — preserved resting place of Vietnam's revolutionary leader
- **Train Street** — narrow residential alley where trains pass within inches of café tables

#### Ha Long Bay
- UNESCO World Heritage Site with over 1,600 limestone islands
- Overnight cruises are strongly recommended for the full experience
- Kayaking through sea caves is a popular activity; book through reputable operators

#### Hoi An
- **Ancient Town** — UNESCO-listed historic trading port with well-preserved merchant houses and lantern-lit canals
- **An Bang Beach** — quieter alternative to Cua Dai beach, 5 km from the Ancient Town
- **Tailors** — Hoi An is famous for made-to-measure clothing; allow 24–48 hours for fitting and production

### Practical Tips
- Vietnamese dong (VND) is the currency; USD and credit cards accepted in tourist areas
- Street food is safe and excellent — follow the locals to the busiest stalls
- Motorbike taxis (xe om) and Grab Bike are fast options in city traffic
